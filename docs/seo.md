## **2. SEO Fundamentals (Core Concepts)**

### **2.1 What is SEO?**

* SEO = Search Engine Optimization — optimizing content & site to rank higher in search.
* Core pillars:

  * Keywords
  * On-page SEO
  * Off-page (backlinks)
  * Technical SEO
    **Key reference:** Complete SEO Course by Ahrefs. ([YouTube][1])

### **2.2 How Google Ranks Content**

* Keywords + relevance to user intent
* Backlinks from authoritative domains
* Technical health (speed, crawlability)
* Engagement metrics (CTR, dwell time)
* Freshness and topical relevance

---

## **3. Keyword Research & Search Intent**

### **3.1 Keyword Discovery**

* Identify target keywords with search demand
* Analyze difficulty & intent before targeting
  **Actionable steps:**
* Use keyword research tools (Ahrefs, YouTube keyword tools)
* Group keywords by topic and intent
  **Reference:** Ahrefs tutorials teach competitive keyword analysis. ([YouTube][1])

### **3.2 Analyze Search Intent**

* Understand whether query seeks information, product, comparison, etc.
* Create content matching that intent

---

## **4. On-Page SEO Optimization**

### **4.1 Content Structure**

* Clear headings (H1, H2, etc.)
* Use target keyword and related terms
* Answer query comprehensively

### **4.2 Title & Meta Elements**

* Include keyword in title and meta description
* Write compelling descriptions for CTR

### **4.3 Content Quality & Relevance**

* Longer, comprehensive articles often outrank short/weak content
* Keep user engaged with structured information

### **4.4 Internal Linking**

* Link related pages to distribute authority and help indexing
  **Note:** Ahrefs has videos on internal link strategies. ([YouTube][2])

---

## **5. Backlinks & Authority Signals**

### **5.1 Building Backlinks**

* Backlinks from high-authority sites improve ranking potential
* Case studies from Ahrefs show effective link building techniques
  **Strategies:**
* Outreach to relevant blogs
* Guest posting
* Content partnerships

### **5.2 Quality Over Quantity**

* A few high-value links can outperform many low-value ones

---

## **6. Technical SEO Essentials**

### **6.1 Site Speed & UX**

* Faster sites rank better
* Mobile-friendly designs crucial

### **6.2 Crawlability & Indexing**

* Ensure pages can be crawled
* Create clean XML sitemap
* Fix broken links and redirects

### **6.3 Structured Data**

* Use schema markup to enhance search appearance

## **8. YouTube & Video SEO (Relevant to Google Rankings)**

### **8.1 How Video Helps SEO**

* Videos embedded on a page keep users longer
* YouTube content can rank in Google search snippets

### **8.2 Video Optimization**

* Use target keyword in title, description, and tags
* High retention signals strong relevance
  **Note:** Ranking YouTube videos also requires traditional SEO. ([Ahrefs][3])
