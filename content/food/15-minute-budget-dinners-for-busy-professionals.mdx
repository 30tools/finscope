## The Biology of the 15-Minute Meal

To cook this fast, you must bypass the "Prep Phase." You are no longer a chef; you are an **Assembler.**
- **Heat Source:** Use high heat (sautéing) or zero-heat (salads/wraps).
- **Surface Area:** The smaller the food is cut, the faster it cooks. Thinly sliced protein and shredded vegetables are the keys to speed.

## The 5 Rapid-Friction Recipes

### 1. The "Power" Salad (7 Minutes)
- **Base:** A bag of pre-washed spinach or kale.
- **Protein:** Canned tuna, mackerel, or pre-cooked hard-boiled eggs.
- **Fat:** Sliced avocado or a handful of sunflower seeds.
- **Budget Tip:** Build your own vinaigrette in the jar (oil + vinegar + mustard). It takes 30 seconds and costs $0.05 per serving.

### 2. The 10-Minute Stir-Fry
- **Base:** Pre-shredded cabbage (coleslaw mix, no dressing).
- **Protein:** Ground turkey or pork (cooks in 4 minutes).
- **Sauce:** Soy sauce + ginger paste (from a tube) + hot sauce.
- **Workflow:** Brown meat, toss in cabbage, add sauce, cover for 2 minutes. Done.

### 3. Mediterranean Chickpea Wraps (5 Minutes)
- **Base:** Whole wheat tortillas.
- **Inside:** Canned chickpeas (rinsed), hummus, cucumber, and feta.
- **Budget Tip:** Chickpeas are one of the highest-fiber proteins and cost under $1 per can.

### 4. Pan-Seared Salmon & Asparagus (12 Minutes)
- **Base:** A single salmon fillet (buy the frozen individual packs).
- **Side:** Thin asparagus stalks (they cook faster than thick ones).
- **Workflow:** Season salmon, place skin-side down in a hot pan. Throw asparagus in the same pan. Flip after 5 minutes. Serve.

### 5. The "Professional" Egg Scramble (10 Minutes)
- **Base:** 3 Eggs.
- **Add-ins:** Frozen peppers and onions (no chopping needed).
- **Side:** A slice of high-quality toast.
- **Insight:** Eggs are not just for breakfast. They are the most efficient 2026 protein for a late-night dinner.

## 3 Tactics for Zero-Friction Cooking

### 1. The "Frozen Aromatics" Hack
Chipping frozen onions, garlic, and ginger is the #1 time-sink. Buy these pre-minced in jars or frozen cubes. The "Convenience Tax" on a \$3 jar of garlic is worth it if it prevents a \$35 DoorDash order.

### 2. The "Pre-Washed" Strategy
In 2026, time is your most expensive ingredient. If you won't wash a head of lettuce because you're exhausted, buy the pre-washed bag. It’s \$1 more, but it ensures you actually eat the salad instead of throwing it away.

### 3. Parallel Cleanup
If you have 4 minutes for the eggs to cook, spend those 4 minutes putting the ingredients back in the fridge. By the time you sit down to eat, the kitchen is already clean.
