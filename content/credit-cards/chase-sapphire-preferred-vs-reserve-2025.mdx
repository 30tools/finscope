# Chase Sapphire Preferred vs Reserve: The 2025 Showdown

If you are in the US and love travel, you likely have one of these cards in your walletâ€”or you're planning to get one.

The **Chase Sapphire Preferred (CSP)** and **Chase Sapphire Reserve (CSR)** are the titans of the travel rewards world. But with the Reserve's annual fee now sitting at **$550**, many are asking: *Is it still worth it?*

## At a Glance

| Feature | Chase Sapphire Preferred | Chase Sapphire Reserve |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Annual Fee** | $95 | $550 |
| **Welcome Bonus** | 60,000 Points | 60,000 Points |
| **Travel Credit** | $50 (Hotel) | $300 (Any Travel) |
| **Earning Rate** | 2x Travel, 3x Dining | 3x Travel, 3x Dining |
| **Point Value** | 1.25 cents (Travel Portal) | 1.5 cents (Travel Portal) |
| **Lounge Access** | None | Priority Pass Select |

## The Case for the Preferred ($95)

For most beginners, the **CSP** is the winner.
1.  **Low Risk**: The $95 fee is negligible compared to the value of the 60,000 bonus points (worth ~$750).
2.  **Great Transfer Partners**: You can transfer points 1:1 to Hyatt, United, and British Airways.
3.  **Solid Insurance**: Primary rental car insurance coverage is included.

## The Case for the Reserve ($550)

The **CSR** is for the frequent flyer.
1.  **Effective Fee is $250**: The $300 travel credit applies automatically to *any* travel (Uber, flights, subway), effectively bringing the fee down to $250.
2.  **Lounge Access**: Access to Priority Pass lounges and the exclusive **Chase Sapphire Lounges** (which are stunning).
3.  **50% Boost**: Points are worth 50% more when booked through Chase Ultimate Rewards.

> **Verdict**: If you visit an airport lounge more than 3 times a year, get the **Reserve**. Otherwise, stick to the **Preferred**.
