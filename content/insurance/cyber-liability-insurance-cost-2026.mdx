# Cyber Liability Insurance 2026: The Cost of Safety

In 2026, Hackers don't just target Chase Bank. They target the local dentist, the e-commerce shop, and the accountant.
Why? Because small [business](https://unstory.app/business)es have weak security.

A data breach costs an average of **$200,000** to fix. Cyber Insurance is the only thing standing between a hack and bankruptcy. Here is what it costs today.

## Average Cost in 2026

For a small [business](https://unstory.app/business) ($1M revenue, &lt;10 employees), Cyber Liability Insurance typically costs:
*   **$1,500 - $2,200 per year** ($145/month).

**Factors that Spike the Price:**
1.  **Record Count:** Do you store 100 SSNs or 100,000? More data = Higher Price.
2.  **Industry:** [Health](https://unstory.app/health)care and Finance pay 30% more (high regulatory fines).
3.  **Security:** No MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)? **Declined.** In 2026, most carriers won't even quote you if you don't have MFA enabled on email.

---

## What Does It Actually Cover?

It covers the "Oh Sh*t" moments:
1.  **Ransomware Payments:** If hackers lock your files, insurance may negotiate and pay the ransom (controversial, but covered by many policies).
2.  **Forensics:** Hiring the IT team to find out *how* they got in ($300/hr).
3.  **Notification:** The legal cost of writing letters to every customer telling them their data was stolen.
4.  **Business Interruption:** Reimbursing your lost profit for the 3 days your website was down.

---

## First-Party vs. Third-Party
*   **First-Party:** Pays *your* costs (Data recovery, PR firm).
*   **Third-Party:** Pays *lawsuits* if your customers sue you for losing their data.
*   *Verdict:* You need **both**.

## Who are the Top Carriers?
*   **Coalition:** [Tech](https://unstory.app/tech)-forward. They scan your website for liabilities *before* insuring you.
*   **Hiscox:** Good for micro-businesses.
*   **Chubb:** Premium carrier for larger firms.
