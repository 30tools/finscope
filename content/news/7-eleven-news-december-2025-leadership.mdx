# 7-Eleven Shakeup: CEO Retires as Brand Pushes New Food Strategy

As 2025 comes to a close, convenience giant **7-Eleven** is undergoing a massive transformation. From the boardroom to the snack aisle, big changes are happening.

## Leadership Change
After two decades at the helm, longtime CEO **Joe DePinto** is retiring.
*   **The Legacy**: DePinto guided the company through massive growth and the pandemic.
*   **The New Guard**: Interim co-CEOs Stanley Reynolds and Douglas Rosencrans have been appointed to steer the ship as the company navigates a potential IPO.

## Food Innovation
The biggest news for customers is the menu overhaul. 7-Eleven is finally listening to fans of its international stores.
*   **Japanese Egg Salad Sandwich**: The cult-favorite item from 7-Eleven Japan has officially launched in U.S. stores. It features milk bread and the specific Japanese mayo recipe that made it famous.
*   **Fresh Food Push**: The brand is aggressively trying to shed its "gas station food" image by introducing more fresh, high-quality grab-and-go options.

## What's Next?
Rumors of a North American IPO persist. With the leadership change and food strategy pivot, 2026 looks to be a pivotal year for the chain.
