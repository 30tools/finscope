# Nikki Glaser's Return: The Roaster-in-Chief Takes the Stage Again

**Dec 29, 2025:** After a polarizing, yet undeniably viral performance in 2025, **Nikki Glaser** is returning to host the **83rd Annual Golden Globe Awards**. The decision by CBS and the HFPA to bring her back signals a desire for the "roast" energy that once defined the Rickey Gervais era.

## Why Nikki Worked
Awards shows have struggled with ratings for years. Glaser, fresh off her legendary performance at the *Roast of Tom Brady*, brought a level of unapologetic, sharp-witted honesty that cut through the typical Hollywood self-congratulation. 
Her 2025 monologue—which included a particularly biting joke about streaming service password sharing—went viral on TikTok, reaching the younger demographic the Globes desperately need.

## What to Expect in 2026
If her recent stand-up tour is any indication, no one is safe.
1.  **The AI Roast:** Expect plenty of jokes about AI actors, deepfakes, and the anxiety surrounding the tech takeover of cinema.
2.  **Streaming Wars:** With Netflix and NEON dominating nominations, Glaser is likely to poke fun at the sheer number of subscriptions the average viewer needs to see all the nominees.
3.  **The "One Battle After Another" Length:** At nearly 3 hours, Paul Thomas Anderson's film is an easy target for jokes about short attention spans.

## The Risk Factor
Glaser’s humor is "blue" and "bold." While the Globes are famous for their loose, alcohol-fueled atmosphere, there is always the risk of a joke landing poorly (see: Jo Koy). However, Glaser's professional experience in roasting ensures she knows exactly where the line is—and how to jump over it.

## Conclusion
Whether you love her or hate her, Nikki Glaser makes the Golden Globes **must-watch TV**. In an era of sanitized media, a little bit of bite is exactly what the doctor ordered.
